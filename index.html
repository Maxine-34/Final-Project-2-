Roots of Change: A Future for Mother Nature

Brief Background

SDG 15, Life on Land, aims to reverse the damage that humans have caused to the planet, particularly to terrestrial life. It saves and restores ecosystems and the life in them, promoting sustainability and preservation to save both ourselves and other species. SDG 15 focuses on reversing the following problems: desertification, biodiversity loss, land degradation, and other related issues affecting terrestrial life.

My advocacy not only focuses on the SDG in general, but more specifically on saving plant biodiversity by planting trees, especially in the Philippines. Our planet is home to many beautiful flora, each contributing a role in our ecosystems and even our daily lives. Unfortunately, many of them are endangered, including in places like the Philippines. In the previous year, it was reported that out of the 46,337 species studied, approximately 58% of them are threatened flowering plants, with an estimated 1% being other plants (Statista & Salas, 2025). Meanwhile, out of an estimated 50,000 plant species in the Philippines(UNDP, 2023), 984 species of flora are threatened as of 2024 (Department of Environment and Natural Resources, 2024). Many of these species are endemic to the country, and as Filipinos, we must protect them by facing problems such as habitat loss, which can be the leading cause of endangerment (Population Education, 2021).

Habitat loss and deforestation are huge causes of endangerment. Forests provide a home to approximately 80% of species (UNDP, n.d.). The biodiversity loss of flora can cause a significant negative effect on our ecosystems, which may even affect our lives. Thus, we must do what we can to preserve not only the beauty of these species, but the stability and significance of our environment.

The Philippines faces many threats to its flora, one of them being endangerment and related issues such as deforestation and habitat loss. Thus, many solutions have been made by the government and multiple organizations with their own advocacies.

Many projects and organizations have been created to deal with the aforementioned problems. One example is the “Protect Wildlife Project” made in 2016, which focuses on addressing the dangers caused to wildlife while tackling issues related to biodiversity loss, such as deforestation and habitat loss, through many ways, such as raising awareness and changing minds, and the enforcement of laws (Conservation International, n.d.). Another recent significant situation is the Climate Change Commission (CCC) showing the importance of biodiversity in our country’s ecosystems. They suggested saving these habitats from illegal activity and protecting them through close monitoring (Climate Change Commission, 2024).

These programs and discussions show that as united Filipinos, we can address problems concerning our home, especially the biodiversity and ecosystems of our country.

As a student, I am taught to care about the environment and the amazing things it holds. Seeing such beautiful and unique species be threatened or be on the verge of extinction is a saddening sight. Thus, I have decided to come up with this advocacy to save both us and the flora biodiversity. It not only helps recover what was lost but also spreads awareness to other people. Many students like me may also be interested or would like to speak up about such problems affecting our environments.

Silence is ignorance—Saving our flora means saving our future!

Ignorance is deciding not to do anything about our threatened flora. Being silent prevents awareness about this problem, leading it to further worsen. We should not let the extinction and endangerment of our plants stay hidden. Thus, we must spread awareness and show the true importance of our plant diversity. Saving our flora not only preserves our planet’s natural environments, but also helps our daily lives and future in some way as they are important in various resources and places (we must not exploit the resources it provides though).
